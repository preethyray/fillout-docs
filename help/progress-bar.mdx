## What is a progress bar

A **progress bar** lets respondents know how much of the form have been completed and how much remains. By default, respondents will see a progress bar at top of the form.

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot of the Form designer tab of a form](/images/Screenshot2025-02-25at4.57.47PM.png)
</Frame>

## How to change the color of the standard progress bar

Click `Theme` in the upper left area to open the **Form designer**.

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot of the Form designer tab of a form](/images/Screenshot2025-02-25at4.49.16PM.png)
</Frame>

Click the `Primary` color picker to choose your desired color.

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot of the Form designer tab of a form](/images/Screenshot2025-02-25at5.00.34PM.png)
</Frame>

## How to add a multi-step progress bar

On the left-hand panel of your form, click `Progress bar`.

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot of the Form designer tab of a form](/images/Screenshot2025-02-25at4.53.04PM.png)
</Frame>

Click the **progress bar** field to customize it. This will open the right-hand panel where you can change its color, rename the current steps, and add new steps. You can add up to five steps.

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot of the Form designer tab of a form](/images/Screenshot2025-02-25at4.53.51PM.png)
</Frame>

UnderÂ **Current page progress**, choose which step each page should go into. Simply go to any page (except Cover and Ending) and match it to a step.

That's it\! `Publish` your form and share the link.

## How to remove the progress bar

Click **Settings** in the top menu followed by `Form behavior` in the left side. Untoggle the `Show progress bar` switch.

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot of the Form designer tab of a form](/images/Screenshot2025-02-25at4.50.39PM.png)
</Frame>
